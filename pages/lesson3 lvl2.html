THE 3-STEP BUILD PROCESS

Build Your Orchestrator in Three Parts

No coding required. You're defining rules in plain English and connecting them to your existing tools.

1
BUILD THE ANALYST RULES
Define what signals to watch: "engagement score below 30 for 7+ days", "renewal date in next 45 days", "support ticket unresolved for 48 hours". Each becomes a trigger condition.
2
BUILD THE ORCHESTRATION MAP
Connect each signal to a workflow: if Analyst flags X, then trigger workflow Y. Example: "renewal in 30 days + usage high" → "expansion offer workflow". Simple IF-THEN logic.
3
CONNECT THE EXECUTION ENGINE
Build the actual workflow sequence: Step 1 = send personalized email. Step 2 = wait 3 days. Step 3 = create task for account manager. Step 4 = update CRM field to "in-workflow".
💡
THE KEY INSIGHT
Start with one signal and one workflow. Don't try to orchestrate everything on day one. Pick your highest-value trigger — usually churn risk or renewal opportunity — and build that first.

💎 KEY IDEAS
REMEMBER THESE FOUR

The Four Principles That Make Orchestration Work

ORCHESTRATION PRINCIPLES — v2.0
01
PRINCIPLE 1 — SIGNALS MUST BE SPECIFIC
Don't trigger on vague conditions like "customer seems disengaged". Trigger on measurable signals: "login frequency dropped 40% in 14 days" or "support tickets increased 3x this month".
02
PRINCIPLE 2 — WORKFLOWS MUST BE COMPLETE
Each workflow should run end-to-end without human input. If a step requires manual decision-making, the workflow isn't ready for automation yet.
03
PRINCIPLE 3 — START SIMPLE, EXPAND GRADUALLY
Launch with one trigger and one workflow. Master that. Then add the next highest-value signal. Trying to orchestrate everything at once leads to broken automation.
04
PRINCIPLE 4 — FEEDBACK IMPROVES THE SYSTEM
Log every execution. Review what worked and what didn't. Adjust signal thresholds and workflow steps based on real outcomes, not assumptions.
