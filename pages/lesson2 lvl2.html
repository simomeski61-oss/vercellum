🧩 COMPONENTS
THE FOUR BUILDING BLOCKS

Your Support Agent Has 4 Components — Click Each One to Understand It

Each component is a module your agent uses. Together, they create a seamless support pipeline. Individually, each one is simple enough to explain to a ten-year-old. Click any card to see what it does in your business.

🧠
1
1. INTENT
What does the customer actually need?
CORE COMPONENT
The Intent module reads every incoming message and classifies it: Is this a billing question? A delivery enquiry? A complaint? A technical issue? A refund request? By detecting intent instantly, the agent knows exactly which part of your knowledge base to search — and whether a human needs to be involved.
💡 Real example: A customer writes "Where is my order?" The agent detects delivery-tracking intent, searches your order management system, and replies with a live tracking link — without any human input.
📚
2
2. KNOWLEDGE BASE
Your answers, always available
CORE COMPONENT
The Knowledge Base is a document you build once — your FAQs, return policy, pricing, delivery times, product specs, troubleshooting steps. The agent searches it every time a question comes in. When a match is found, it generates a clear, friendly answer in your brand voice. Build it once. It answers forever.
💡 Real example: Your return policy is 30 days no questions asked. You put it in the knowledge base. Now every "how do I return this?" question is answered instantly and consistently — with the right policy every time.
💬
3
3. RESPOND
The answer, delivered instantly
CORE COMPONENT
The Respond module takes the answer from your knowledge base and turns it into a complete, on-brand reply — personalised with the customer's name, relevant to their specific situation. It sends the reply automatically and logs the interaction in your CRM. Fast, accurate, and consistent — every single time.
💡 Real example: A customer asks about your pricing at midnight on a Friday. Within 15 seconds, they have a personalised breakdown of your packages in their inbox — before you've even seen the message.
🚨
4
4. ESCALATE
When humans need to step in
CORE COMPONENT
Not every question has a clean answer. When the agent detects a complex issue, an emotional complaint, a legal matter, or a question outside its knowledge base, it escalates immediately. The customer is told a human will be in touch. Your team gets a full summary of the conversation — so they don't start from scratch. Escalation builds trust — done right.
💡 Real example: A customer writes an angry message about a faulty product. The agent detects emotional language, flags it as a complaint requiring human response, and notifies your team with the full context — within 30 seconds of receipt.

2
PHASE 2 n8n / Make
BUILD IN N8N — Connect, Configure, Deploy
⏱ Estimated: 1–2 hours
Set up a workflow: incoming email or chat triggers the agent, which searches the knowledge base for a matching answer, generates a personalised reply, and sends it. Add a second branch: if confidence is low or the topic is sensitive, route to your human support inbox.
When a customer message arrives: (1) Classify intent — billing / delivery / product / complaint / other. (2) Search knowledge base for relevant answer. (3) If match found (confidence > 75%): generate reply using the answer, personalise with customer name, send automatically. (4) If no match or complaint detected: escalate to human with full message context and suggested response draft.
3
PHASE 3 Test Protocol
TEST YOUR AGENT — Before Going Live
⏱ Estimated: 30–60 min
Send 10 test messages that cover your most common question types. Verify the agent replies correctly, escalates when it should, and uses the right tone. Then run a live pilot for one week — with you reviewing every reply before it sends — before switching to full automation.
Test the agent with the following scenarios and verify correct behaviour: (1) "What is your return policy?" → expect: accurate policy reply, no escalation. (2) "I am very unhappy with my order and want to speak to a manager." → expect: escalation triggered, empathetic holding message sent. (3) "Do you offer corporate accounts?" → expect: escalation if not in knowledge base. Log all test results and refine knowledge base before going live.

🔑 KEY INSIGHTS
THREE THINGS TO REMEMBER FOREVER

The 3 Principles Behind Every Effective Support Agent

01
AI = AN ASSISTANT, NOT A REPLACEMENT
The agent handles what's repetitive and routine — freeing your team for what requires genuine human judgment, empathy, and relationship. The goal is not to replace your support function. It's to protect your team's time and your customers' patience.
02
80% OF QUESTIONS = 10 QUESTIONS
In virtually every SMB, 80% of all support questions are variations of the same 10 questions. Build a knowledge base that answers those 10 perfectly, and you've automated the vast majority of your support workload — immediately, on day one.
03
ESCALATION BUILDS TRUST
Customers don't expect AI to answer everything. What they hate is being stuck in a loop that never reaches a human. An agent that escalates gracefully — with a clear message and a fast human follow-up — actually increases customer satisfaction compared to human-only support.
🔑
THE COMPOUNDING TRUTH

These three principles — AI as assistant, focus on the 80%, graceful escalation — apply to every support system you'll ever build. Clear scope. Accurate answers. Human when it counts. Master this pattern and your customers will trust your business more, not less.
