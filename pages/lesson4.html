Every AI Agent

Click each component below to understand how it works in detail. These four pieces form the complete architecture of any agent you'll ever build.

👁️
01 · Sense
The agent perceives incoming signals from the world

The Sense layer is your agent's perception system. It continuously monitors one or more data sources — emails, web forms, CRM updates, calendar events, API feeds — and detects events that match its watch conditions. Without a well-defined Sense layer, the agent cannot know when to act.

Email Agent Example
Sense layer monitors Gmail inbox every 60 seconds. When a new email arrives, it reads the sender, subject, and body and passes it to the Plan layer for evaluation.
🧠
02 · Plan
The decision center — evaluating signals against goals

The Plan layer is the decision engine. It takes raw data from Sense and evaluates it against your rules and goals to determine the right action. This is where your business logic lives — the if/then reasoning that makes the agent genuinely useful.

Email Agent Example
Plan evaluates the email: Does it contain pricing keywords? Is this a known customer? Which response template applies? It outputs a specific action instruction to the Act layer.
⚡
03 · Act
Autonomous execution — the agent does the work

The Act layer is where your agent executes. It carries out the Plan layer's instruction — sending an email, updating a CRM record, notifying a team member, or triggering a downstream workflow. Act operates autonomously, without waiting for human input on each task.

Email Agent Example
Act sends a personalized pricing reply, creates a new lead record in your CRM, and adds a follow-up task for your sales team due tomorrow at 9am.
📊
04 · Respond
Reports results, logs activity, and closes the loop

The Respond layer closes the loop. It records what the agent did, reports outcomes to stakeholders, and feeds data back into Sense so the next cycle begins. Without Respond, your agent is a black box — you cannot audit, improve, or trust it.

Email Agent Example
Respond logs the interaction in your dashboard, sends a Slack notification ("Lead handled: John Smith"), and updates the CRM record with timestamp and action summary.

🛡️ SECTION 4
AI SAFETY FRAMEWORK

The 4 Rules That Keep
Your Agent Under Control

Safety isn't a constraint on your agent's capability — it's what makes your agent trustworthy enough to run unsupervised. Every principle below directly prevents one of the three root causes of agent failure.

🪪
Identity

Give your agent its own dedicated account — a separate email address, a dedicated login, a distinct API key. Never run your agent under your personal credentials. If your agent is ever compromised or makes a mistake, you need clear separation between its actions and yours.

Rule: One agent = one identity
🔑
Least Privilege

Grant your agent only the permissions it needs to complete its specific task — and nothing more. An email agent needs to read and send emails. It does not need access to your billing system, your full CRM, or your document library.

Rule: Minimum access, maximum control
🧑‍✈️
Human-in-the-Loop

For high-stakes actions — sending discount offers, issuing refunds, cancelling orders, modifying contracts — require a human approval step before the agent executes. Let the agent do the legwork; let you make the final call.

Rule: High stakes = human approval
📝
Logging

Record every action your agent takes — what it sensed, what it decided, what it executed, and the outcome. Logs let you audit, improve, and defend your agent's behaviour. They also help you spot drift before it becomes a problem.

Rule: If it's not logged, it didn't happen
